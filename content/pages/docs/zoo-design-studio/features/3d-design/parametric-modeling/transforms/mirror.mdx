Mirror reflects selected 3D geometry across a reference plane, edge, axis, or sketch segment. Use
Mirror for symmetric parts, mirrored design variants, and creating paired surface or solid bodies
without manually rebuilding the second side.

## Options

### Objects
Select the surface or solid bodies you want to mirror. Multiple bodies can be mirrored together.

### Across
Choose the geometry that defines the mirror reference. This can be a default plane, a construction
plane, an edge, an axis, or a sketch segment.

## Design Applications

### Symmetric Parts
Build one side of a model, then mirror it across a center plane:
- Create left and right halves from a single modeled side
- Keep paired features aligned around a centerline
- Reduce repeated work on symmetric housings, brackets, and covers

### Surface Modeling
Reflect surface bodies while preserving their shape relationships:
- Create paired styling surfaces
- Mirror one side of a surface layout before joining or trimming
- Keep complex curvature consistent across a model centerline

### Layout And Variants
Generate opposing or handed geometry:
- Create left-hand and right-hand versions
- Place matching components on opposite sides of an assembly
- Explore mirrored alternatives without redrawing base geometry

Behind the scenes, Mirror maps to KCL's
[`mirror3d`](/docs/kcl-std/functions/std-transform-mirror3d) function.
